Music Hardware

PC

A personal computer used to as a verseatile tools which can be used for many task and is also a place where they can edit, create, mixs, and produce music.

MIDI controller

A MIDI controller is a device that sends musical data to computers or instruments, usually through keys, pads, or knobs. It doesn’t make sound on its own but controls virtual instruments and software.

Audio interface

This is used if you want to record music to your computer software using microphone, keyboard or guitar. It is used to connect your computer to your studio monitor speaker.

Headphones

Headphones are wearable speakers that let users listen to audio privately. Originally invented for telephone operators, they later became essential in music production and everyday listening.

Studio monitors

Studio monitors are speakers designed for accurate and uncolored sound playback. They help music producers hear every detail in a mix to make precise adjustments.

Microphones & microphone gear

An industry-standard DAW used for high-end audio production and film scoring. Microphones convert sound into electrical signals for recording or amplification. Paired with gear like stands, pop filters, and preamps, they are essential for capturing clear audio.

Analog Synthesizer

An analog synthesizer is an electronic instrument that generates sound using electrical signals. It became popular in the 1960s for its ability to create unique tones and textures in music.

Miscellaneous percussion

To keep rhythm and make special sounds also adding color and excitement to the music through using early rhythm instruments like shakers, drums, and bells. These were some of the first tools used to create music in ancient cultures.

Innovative Instrument

Mood Adapt music player

Users would enjoy a unique listening experience tailored specifically to their emotions and activities. Such devices could revolutionize how we experience music by making it more closely connected to our emotional and physical states.

How it works:

A mood-adaptive music player works by detecting the user's emotional state. It analyzes facial expressions via webcam to identify emotions such as happiness, sadness, anger, or calmness. It can also analyze the voice, examining the user's speech patterns, tone, and pitch to determine emotional cues. This device collects real-time data from the user through various inputs like cameras, microphones, and sensors. Based on the detected mood, it provides personalized music recommendations.
